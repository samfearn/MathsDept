Calculate \[\lim_{x\rightarrow 1}\frac{x^2+x-2}{x^2-x}.\]

 

\(\lim_{x\rightarrow 1}\frac{x^2+x-2}{x^2-x}=\lim_{x\rightarrow 1}\frac{(x+2)(x-1)}{x(x-1)}\)

\(=\lim_{x\rightarrow 1}\frac{x+2}{x}=3.\)

Calculate \[\lim_{x\rightarrow 9}\frac{\sqrt{x}-3}{x-9}.\]

 

\(\lim_{x\rightarrow 9}\frac{\sqrt{x}-3}{x-9} =\lim_{x\rightarrow 9}\frac{(\sqrt{x}-3)(\sqrt{x}+3)}{(x-9)(\sqrt{x}+3)}\)

\(=\lim_{x\rightarrow 9}\frac{x-9}{(x-9)(\sqrt{x}+3)}\)

\(=\lim_{x\rightarrow 9}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}+3}=1/6.\)

Calculate \[\lim_{x\rightarrow 0}\frac{1-\cos 2x}{x}.\]

 

\(\lim_{x\rightarrow 0} \frac{1-\cos 2x}{x}=\lim_{x\rightarrow 0}\frac{2(1-\cos 2x)}{2x}\)

\(=\lim_{u\rightarrow 0}\frac{2(1-\cos u)}{u}=0.\)

Calculate \(\lim_{x\rightarrow \pi/2}\{(x-\pi/2)\tan x\}.\)

 

Set \(u=x-\pi/2\) then \(\lim_{x\rightarrow \pi/2}(x-\pi/2)\tan x =\lim_{u\rightarrow 0}u\tan (u+\pi/2)\)

\(=\lim_{u\rightarrow 0}\frac{u\sin (u+\pi/2)}{\cos (u+\pi/2)} =\lim_{u\rightarrow 0}\frac{-u\cos u}{\sin u}\)

\(=-1,\) where in the last step we used one of our standard trigonometric identities.

Calculate \[\lim_{x\rightarrow \infty}\frac{(x^2+1)^2-(x^2-1)^2}{(x+2)^3-(x+1)^3}.\]

 

\(\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\frac{(x^2+1)^2-(x^2-1)^2}{(x+2)^3-(x+1)^3}= \lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\frac{4x^2}{3x^2+9x+7}\)

\(=\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\frac{4}{3+\frac{9}{x}+\frac{7}{x^2}}\)

\(=4/3.\)

Calculate \(\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}x\sin\frac{1}{x}.\)

 

Setting \(u=1/x\) then \(\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}x\sin\frac{1}{x} =\lim_{u\rightarrow 0^+}\frac{\sin u}{u}\)

\(=1,\) where in the last step we used one of our standard trigonometric identities.

Use the squeezing theorem to calculate \(\lim_{x \rightarrow 0}\tan^2{(\frac{x}{2})}\cos^2{(\frac{2}{x})}\).

 As \(0 \le \cos^2{(\frac{2}{x})} \le 1\), then \(0 \le \tan^2{(\frac{x}{2})}\cos^2{(\frac{2}{x})} \le \tan^2{(\frac{x}{2})} \quad \forall x\ne 0\ \in (-\pi , \pi)\).

Now since \(\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} 0 = 0\) and \(\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \tan(\frac{x}{2}) = 0\),

then by the squeezing theorem, \(\lim_{x \rightarrow 0}\tan^2{(\frac{x}{2})}\cos^2{(\frac{2}{x})} = 0\).
