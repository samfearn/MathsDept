Calculus I, Tutorial Problem Sheet, Week 10

 

Taylor polynomials and series

  1. Calculate the second order Taylor polynomial of \(f(x)=xe^{\sin x}\) about \(x=\pi/2.\)

  2. Calculate the first order Taylor polynomial of \(f(x)=e^{\sin^2 x}\) about \(x=0\) and use this result to show that \(e^{\sin^2 x}\le 1+\frac{3}{2}ex^2.\)

  3. Use Taylor polynomials to estimate the following to within \(0.01\)

    (a) \(\sqrt{e}\) (which is less than 2), (b) \(\sin(0.3),\) (c) \(\log(1.2).\)

  4. Let \(P_n(x)\) be the \(n^{th}\) order Taylor polynomial of the function \(f(x)=\sin x\). Find the least integer \(n\) for which

    (a) \(P_n(1)\) approximates \(\sin(1)\) to within \(0.001\)

    (b) \(P_n(2)\) approximates \(\sin(2)\) to within \(0.001\)

  5. Compute the Taylor series of \(f(x)=x^3+2x+1\) about \(x=2\) and comment on the result.

  6. Compute the third order Taylor polynomial of \(f(x)=\log(1+x)\) about \(x=2\) and give the remainder term in Lagrange form.

  7. Show that \(e^{\sin x}=1+x+\frac{1}{2}x^2+R_2(x)\) where \(|R_2(x)|\le \frac{5}{6}e|x|^3.\)

  8. Use Taylor series to calculate \(\lim_{x\rightarrow 0}\frac{(2+x)^k-2^k}{x},\) where \(k\) is a positive real number.

  9. Calculate the third order Taylor polynomial of \(f(x)=\frac{\log(1+2x)}{1-x}\) about \(x=0\) and use this result to calculate \(\lim_{x\rightarrow 0}\frac{f(x)-2x}{1+x^3-\sqrt{1+x^3}}.\)

 

Fourier series

  1. Simplify the following expressions involving the positive integer \(n\)

    (a)  \(\sin\frac{55\pi}{2}+\sin^2(\frac{n\pi}{2})+\cos\frac{43\pi}{2}+\cos\frac{30\pi}{2},\)

    (b)  \(\cos(n\pi)+\cos(-n\pi),\)

    (c)  \(\frac{1}{n+2}\cos((n+2)\pi)+\frac{1}{n}\cos(n\pi).\)

  2. The function \(f(x)\) has period 2 and is given by \[f(x)=\begin{cases} 1 & \text{if\ } -1<x<0\\ x & \text{if\ } 0\le x<1\\ \end{cases}\] Calculate the Fourier series of \(f(x)\) and state the value that the series converges to when \(x=0.\)

  3. The function \(f(x)\) has period \(2\pi\) and is given by \[f(x)=\begin{cases} 0 & \text{if\ } -\pi<x<0\\ x^2 & \text{if\ } 0\le x<\pi\\ \end{cases}\] Calculate the Fourier series of \(f(x)\) and use this to evaluate the series \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2}\),   \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^2}\),   and  \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{(2n-1)^2}\).

  4. The function \(f(x)\) has period \(4\) and is given by \[f(x)=\begin{cases} 1 & \text{if\ } -2<x<-1\\ 0 & \text{if\ } -1\le x<1\\ 1 & \text{if\ } 1\le x<2\\ \end{cases}\] Calculate the Fourier series of \(f(x).\)

  5. The function \(f(x)\) has period \(2\pi\) and is given by \(f(x)=x^3-\pi^2x\) for \(x\in(-\pi,\pi).\)

    Calculate the Fourier series of \(f(x)\) and apply Parseval’s theorem to calculate \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^6}\).

  6. Calculate the half range cosine series of the function \(f(x)=\sin x\) defined on \((0,\pi).\)

  7. The function \(f(x)\) has period \(4\) and is given by \[f(x)=\begin{cases} -1 & \text{if\ } -2<x<0\\ 1 & \text{if\ } 0< x<2\\ \end{cases}\] Calculate the complex form of the Fourier series of \(f(x)\).
